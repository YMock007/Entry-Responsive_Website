Environmental Actions By SP

Singapore Polytechnic (SP) is committed to sustainability and environmental conservation. Below are some of the initiatives and actions taken by SP to promote a greener and more sustainable campus.

# Order Actions Description
1 Conserving natural resources Singapore Polytechnic has ceased using bottled water at events to reduce plastic waste on campus. In 2023, they will install photo-voltaic panels across campus to harness renewable clean energy.
2 Creating liveable spaces SP has installed smart sensors that track indoor air quality and human traffic to keep its surroundings comfy and occupants happy.
3 Promoting environmental awareness/td> SP has a number of initiatives in place to promote environmental awareness among its students, staff and the wider community. These include the SP Environment Club, which organises green campaigns and projects, and the Sustainability Matters programme, which provides students with the opportunity to learn about sustainability and how they can make a difference.
4 Promoting biodiversity Singapore Polytechnic is promoting biodiversity on campus by planting trees, shrubs, and flowers, and by creating habitats for wildlife. The polytechnic has also installed a number of bird feeders and bat boxes, and has created a butterfly garden.
5 Reduced energy consumption Singapore Polytechnic has reduced its energy consumption by 20% since 2008. This has been achieved through a number of initiatives, including installing energy-efficient lighting, upgrading to more efficient air-conditioning systems, and using renewable energy sources such as solar panels.
6 Using rainwater harvesting Singapore Polytechnic collects rainwater from its roofs and uses it to flush toilets and water plants, which reduces the amount of water that is drawn from the city's reservoirs.
7 Recycling and composting Singapore Polytechnic encourages its students and staff to recycle and compost, which reduces the amount of waste that goes to landfill.
8 Using energy-efficient appliances Singapore Polytechnic has replaced its old appliances with energy-efficient models, which reduces the amount of electricity that the school uses.
Larry Promoting green transportation Singapore Polytechnic encourages its students and staff to use public transportation, bicycles, or walk to school, which reduces the amount of pollution that is caused by cars.
9 Educated the community Singapore Polytechnic has educated the community about sustainability through a number of initiatives, including hosting sustainability workshops, publishing sustainability reports, and participating in sustainability events.

12 4 Green Mark Platinum Buildings T18A, T18B, T3B and the SP Sports Arena have been certified Green Mark Platinum with on-going efforts to certify all 22 buildings in SP by 2025.
13 500+ Smart Sensors Many facilities on campus are monitored with smart sensors to track human traffic and monitor air quality for the health and well-being of students and staff.
14 230 Species of Flora SP's lush campus is home to an incredible biodiversity of plants including our majestic Brown Woolly Fig Heritage Trees found near Food Court 1.
15 Conducted research Singapore Polytechnic researchers are conducting research on a variety of sustainability topics, including energy efficiency, water conservation, and waste management. This research helps to develop new technologies and solutions that can be used to reduce the environmental impact of the polytechnic and the wider community.
16 Partnered with organizations Singapore Polytechnic has partnered with a number of organizations, including the National Environment Agency, the Energy Market Authority, and the Water Utilities Authority, to promote sustainability. These partnerships help to share knowledge and resources, and to develop and implement new sustainability initiatives.
17 Set Goals Singapore Polytechnic has set a number of sustainability goals, including reducing its energy consumption by 30% by 2030, reducing its water consumption by 20% by 2030, and reducing its waste production by 15% by 2030. These goals help to guide the polytechnic's sustainability efforts and to ensure that it is making progress towards a more sustainable future.
